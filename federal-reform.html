Federal Gov't Reform

An approach for fiscal responsibility at all levels of government.

I am a fiscal conservative. I am a strong believer in balanced budgets, especially for governments. My undergraduate degree is in Accounting, with a Masters in Business Administration. I have a few ideas to help put our Federal Government on track for financial success.

Guiding Principle
"Keep your eye on one thing and one thing only: how much government is spending, because that's the true tax."

— Milton Friedman, Economist

Step One: Overhaul the Tax Code

Replace all federal taxes with a single 10% National Sales Tax on consumer purchases (groceries exempt). One tax. One rate. No loopholes.

Step Two: Cut Spending to Match Revenue

Cut total government outlays to match the revenue generated by the 10% National Sales Tax — approximately $1.8 trillion annually.

Step Three: Pay Off All Debts

Allocate 50% of taxes collected into paying off debts — including Social Security, Medicare, and Medicaid obligations. When all debt is paid off, lower tax rates to 5%.
